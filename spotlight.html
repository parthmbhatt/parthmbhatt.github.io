SpotLight

Summary

Research

Our team started by interviewing 35 different stakeholders in the dining hall space including service staff, executive chefs, Northwestern's dietitian, operation managers, and students.

 

We used this data to create a stakeholder map of the current and envisioned state of anaphylaxis response in dining halls.

An interesting insight we uncovered was that dining hall staff were not only not empowered, but actively dissuaded from offering any sort of help or advice that might be interpreted as 'medical advice'. This included even simple things like offering orange juice to a student as a counter for low blood sugar.

 

Thus we set our goals to empower bystanders and dining hall staff to act in the case of an anaphylaxis emergency.

 

We used the data we had gathered to create a service blueprint of what the new offering would look like across the dining hall environment, keeping our goals in mind.

Ideation

We considered several solutions, narrowed them down to two that we thought might work.

 

EpiNet was a phone based network on campus that aspired to get students with Epi pens to respond to anaphylaxis emergencies

 

 

Epibox was an emergency response platform that allowed people to react in the case of Anaphylaxis. The Epibox contains epinephrine auto injectors that anyone can use and automatically communicates with first responders. The Epibox is supported by a training session for campus staff and is most often located in or near dining locations on campus.

 

We tested both these concepts, and found the second to be more effective at empowering individuals to act

Emergency Simulations

To test the Epibox we set up a simulation of a dining hall and created a mock anaphylaxis attack and watched the students' reactions. From our learnings we concluded that the placement of this solution in dining halls would be effective in reducing the time between attack and action, potentially saving someone's life.

Final Deliverables

Our Class's final deliverable to our Client was SpotLight, a portfolio of solutions. SpotLight consisted of the work done by our entire class, a total of 5 teams. Our Particular deliverables included all our research data, the concept of the Epi Box-an emergency fixture containing Epinephrine autoinjectors that informs the user on how to use the devices, the data regarding our failed concept Epi Net, a text-based, crowdsourced emergency response system, and a guideline for how to conduct an emergency simulation that can be rolled out to other campuses.
