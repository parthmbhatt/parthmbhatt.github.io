SlipGrip Phone Case

Summary

Problem Definition

Auto Rickshaws are one of the most prevalent forms of transport in India. they are quick, small, and highly maneuverable. However the body of an Auto Rickshaw is no more than a thin metal three-wheeled chassis with a canvas roof. Even with the bulk of the vehicle near the bottom, the center of gravity of the Auto Rickshaw is fairly high when loaded, and it's low mass leads to swerving and large vibrations. When the condition of roads in many rain-prone areas (such as Mumbai) is taken into account, it makes for a dangerous place to use one's phone.

 

With this problem in mind I set out to design a solution that would keep my phone safe while using it inside an Auto Rickshaw

Ideation

I discarded any ideas that would require modification to the Rickshaw itself because the rickshaw-walla isn't quite that bothered about the safety of the passenger's phone.

 

After some thought I settled on a phone accessory that would help keep it secured to the user's hand, and came up with some ideas of how the device might look/work

One particular idea stuck with me because if it's simplicity and the fact that it was fairly unobtrusive, and could fit fingers of any size.

 

The phone case in reference would have a slot on it's back where a sliding tab would sit, with an elastic strap fastened between the tab and the top of the slot. When the User needed it, they could slide the tab up the required amount (with it's detented motion) and slip their middle or index finger into the strap, thus securing the phone.

CAD

I then modeled the case in SolidWorks by modifying a generic case for the iPhone 4s (I used the iPhone because of it's straight-backed geometry as compared to other kinds of phones)

 

This would be the phone in it's closed position

When the tab would be pushed upwards, the strap would pop out, allowing the user to slip their finger into it.

 

I imagined the Case of the phone would be made from a matte-finish high Durometer value Polyurethane to allow the plastic tab to slide smoothly and to ensure the user had a decent grip even when not using the tab
