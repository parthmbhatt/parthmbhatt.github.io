At HLB I was part of a holistic design process that took into consideration the needs of all the stakeholders.

 

Once Primary design research had been used to create solid Product Definitions, the Engineering and Industrial Design teams worked in tandem to create the best possible product for our client, following through several stages of product prototyping, testing and iteration followed by DFMA and ID, working closely with suppliers and manufacturers to reduce the chances of any hiccups down the line.

 

Finally, HLB provides support after the release of technical drawings and specifications to manufacturers as needed.

Based on our research and understanding of existing products, we came up with a concept for a new product for our client. We came up with two configurations of the concept that we then modeled and ran CFD analyses on using SolidWorks Flow. This was done to make improvements to the design before prototyping it.

Once we'd made changes to the design based on the CFD results, we proceeded to model the two prototypes in tandem, keeping as many components common between the two to allow for faster and more cost-effective prototyping.

 

The prototypes were created from the Somos Next material using SLA, and laser cutting acrylic. We used off-the shelf parts wherever applicable

The two prototypes were then retro-fitted onto the body of an existing product, and then their performance was tested with great care and precision to decide which one performed better

After performing the tests and reporting the results back to our client, we eliminated one of the concepts and decided to proceed with the other.

Once the concept was finalized we started creating the CAD for the final concept, this time keeping in mind manufacturability (for injection molding) and assembly sequence/orientation. I was introduced to practical applications of concepts such as drafts, mold shut-offs, filleting, material reduction, ribbing, coring, Poka-Yoke, etc.

The project is under NDA till it is rolled out on shelves. HLB is currently finalizing technical drawings to hand off to our clients' manufacturing firm in China.
