HLB

At HLB I was part of a 4-person engineering team handling multiple large projects for our clients. This is the material regarding the largest of those projects - An electro mechanical device to be used on construction sites.

 

It involved new product development, Engineering Analysis, CFD simulations, Rapid Prototyping, and DFMA.

Ideation and verification

Based on our research and understanding of existing products, the team came up with a concept for a new product for our client.

 

I ran CFD simulations on the concepts for analysis

Prototyping

After making changes to the design based on the CFD results, we modeled two prototypes in tandem, keeping as many components common between the two to allow for faster and more cost-effective prototyping.

 

Testing

The two prototypes were then retro-fitted onto the body of an existing product, and then their performance was tested with great care and precision to decide which one performed better

After performing the tests and reporting the results back to our client, we eliminated one of the concepts and decided to proceed with the other.

DFMA using Solidworks

Once the concept was finalized we created the CAD for the final concept, which was to be injection molded and assembled top-down.

 

 

I was introduced to practical applications of manufacturability concepts such as drafts, mold shut-offs, filleting, material reduction, ribbing, coring, Poka-Yoke, etc.

The project is under NDA till it is rolled out on shelves. HLB is currently finalizing technical drawings to hand off to our clients' manufacturing firm in China. I'm one of the inventors on the patent that's being filed
