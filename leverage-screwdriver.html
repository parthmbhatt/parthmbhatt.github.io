I often enjoy opening appliances or devices that have fallen into disuse, these devices often have fasteners that are machine-screwed and hence difficult to undo by simple rotation around the screwdriver's axis. With this project I tried to create a screwdriver that would allow me to open the toughest screws.

The two screwdrivers I modelled allow the user to spin the screwdriver quickly in their native positions and apply a high torque in their secondary positions

I started with my problem definition: "Certain screws are very difficult to undo when disassembling appliances"

 

And then came up with a set of requirements for my design:

 

  • It must be usable as a regular screwdriver
  • It must allow for quick rotation when less torque is required
  • It must allow for extra leverage when a tight screw is encountered.

With these directives in mind I sketched out a number of ideas, some good, some not so good.

 

I decided to go forward with the most interesting ideas rather than the most feasible (and perhaps more simple) ones

The first concept used a 45° swivel joint concept to swivel to a 90° angle for greater torque

Once the screwdriver is in its secondary state, the mobile handle rides on rails to make a T shape that provides greater leverage for turning

The second concept was one with arms that folded out if the user applied force to the top of the screwdriver
