MindDraw

This device, created under the theme 'Wabi-Sabi' attempted to give users a unique method to create doodles, by using biosignals. The final prototype used EMG signals to control it. This 5 day project involved Mechanism design, Mechatronics design, and CAD.

As our introductory challenge at EDI, our class were asked to build a project themed around Wabi-Sabi, the concept of finding beauty in imperfection. I decided to create a platform for expression that tapped directly into the biosignals of users to create an expression unique to the user but completely out of their voluntary control.

Prototyping

Created a 5 bar linkage to draw seemingly random patterns

 

Using CAD, I ensured that the mechanism wouldn't run into itself, as well as the dimensions I'd need to limit the draw area to A4 size

I controlled the speed of the DC motors by PWM-ing transistor switches to achieve variable power output.

EEG was out of my budget, so I settled for EMG instead.

Testing

This being my first foray into an integrated electromechanical system, I was overjoyed when my contraption actually worked!

 

 

(There's an electrode on my forehead because that's the best place I could find for the ground electrode)

To add some final touches, I used magnets to temporarily hold the piece of paper in place.

 

One drawback of the project was that it would map the motor speed values based on a fixed, rather than a dynamic range.

 

This meant that different users would have different responses relative to the machine, rather than relative to their own EMG signal
