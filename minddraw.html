As our introductory challenge at EDI, our class were asked to build a project themed around Wabi-Sabi, the concept of finding beauty in the unfinished, or the imperfect. I decided to create a platform for expression that tapped directly into the biosignals of users to create an expression unique to the user but completely out of their voluntary control.

I created a 5 bar linkage, inspired by        this video, and I had the motors running at complementary speeds, determined by the biosignal of choice (originally planned to be EEG).

 

Using CAD, I ensured that the mecahnism wouldn't run into itself, as well as the dimensions I'd need to create 'drawings' that would fit on A4 or Letter size paper.`

I controlled the speed of the DC motors by PWM-ing transistor switches to achieve variable power output.

It so happened that the EEG kit would eat up my entire budget, so I had to settle for EMG instead, using disposable electrodes to pick up minute electrical impulses from muscle activity.

This being my first foray into an integrated electromechanical system, I was overjoyed when my contraption actually worked!

 

 

There's an electrode on my forehead because that's the best place I could find for the ground electrode

To add some final touches, I used magnets to temporarily hold the piece of paper in place.

 

One significant failing of the project was that it would map the motor speed values based on a fixed range, rather than a dynamic one. This meant that different users would have different responses relative to the machine, rather than relative to their own EMG signal (Basically users with more muscle got more dramatic results out of the machine than those with less)
